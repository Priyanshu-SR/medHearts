Maintain a healthy lifestyle to keep it that way:

{% elif result == "HYPERTENSION (Stage-1)" %}

Stage 1 Hypertension Detected.

It's important to monitor your blood pressure and make lifestyle changes:

Dietary Tips

  • Follow a heart-healthy diet, such as the DASH diet.
  • Reduce salt intake to less than 1,500 milligrams per day.
  • Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy products.
  • Limit intake of red meat, sweets, and sugary beverages.
  • Include sources of healthy fats, such as nuts, seeds, and olive oil.

Exercise Routine

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
  • Incorporate strength training exercises at least twice a week.
  • Include activities like brisk walking, jogging, cycling, or swimming.
  • Practice relaxation exercises like yoga or tai chi to manage stress.

General Tips

  • Lose weight if you are overweight or obese.
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol intake to moderate levels.
  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly and keep a record.
  • Consult with a healthcare provider for personalized advice and monitoring.
{% elif result == "HYPERTENSION (Stage-2)" %}

Stage 2 Hypertension Detected.

Consult with a healthcare provider for proper management and treatment:

Dietary Tips

  • Adopt a strict low-sodium diet and avoid high-salt foods.
  • Consume a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Avoid processed foods, canned soups, and fast foods that are high in sodium.
  • Include potassium-rich foods like bananas, potatoes, and spinach.
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol intake.

Exercise Routine

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
  • Incorporate strength training exercises at least twice a week.
  • Activities like walking, swimming, and cycling are beneficial.
  • Practice relaxation exercises like yoga or tai chi to manage stress.

General Tips

  • Monitor your blood pressure at home and keep a record.
  • Take prescribed medications as directed by your healthcare provider.
  • Avoid tobacco products and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Manage stress through healthy activities like yoga, meditation, or hobbies.
{% elif result == "HYPERTENSIVE CRISIS" %}

Hypertensive Crisis Detected. Seek Immediate Medical Attention!

While waiting for medical assistance:

Immediate Actions

  • Stay calm and try to relax.
  • Avoid any stressful activities or situations.
  • Do not consume any food or drink that could raise blood pressure further.
  • Follow any instructions given by your healthcare provider if you have a history of hypertensive crisis.
  • Make sure you have someone with you to assist and monitor your condition until help arrives.

Preventive Measures

  • Work with your healthcare provider to manage your blood pressure effectively.
  • Take prescribed medications consistently and as directed.
  • Follow a strict low-sodium diet and avoid high-salt foods.
  • Maintain a healthy weight through diet and regular exercise.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake.
  • Practice stress management techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
