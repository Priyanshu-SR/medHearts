 Blood Pressure

What is Blood Pressure?

Blood Pressure (BP) is the force exerted by circulating blood against the walls of the body's arteries. It's measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and recorded with two numbers:

  • Systolic Pressure: The pressure when the heart beats while pumping blood.
  • Diastolic Pressure: The pressure when the heart is at rest between beats.

Normal Blood Pressure Range

  • Normal BP: Systolic < 120 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg.
  • Elevated BP: Systolic 120-129 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg.
  • Hypertension Stage 1: Systolic 130-139 mmHg or Diastolic 80-89 mmHg.
  • Hypertension Stage 2: Systolic ≥ 140 mmHg or Diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg.

Causes of High Blood Pressure

  • Primary (Essential) Hypertension: No identifiable cause, develops over many years.
  • Secondary Hypertension: Caused by an underlying condition, such as kidney disease, adrenal gland tumors, thyroid problems, or certain medications.

Symptoms of High Blood Pressure

Often called the "silent killer" because it may not cause symptoms. When symptoms do occur, they can include:

  • Headaches
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nosebleeds

Complications

  • Heart Attack or Stroke: High BP can cause hardening and thickening of the arteries.
  • Aneurysm: Increased BP can cause blood vessels to weaken and bulge.
  • Heart Failure: The heart has to work harder to pump blood.
  • Kidney Damage: Can lead to kidney failure.

Why Monitoring Blood Pressure is Essential

Regular blood pressure monitoring helps in:

  • Early Detection: Identifying potential issues before they escalate.
  • Effective Management: Adjusting lifestyle and treatment plans as needed.
  • Health Monitoring: Tracking changes over time for better health outcomes.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Blood Pressure

  • Maintain a healthy weight and stay active.
  • Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and low in sodium.
  • Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking.
  • Regularly check your blood pressure and follow your healthcare provider’s advice.
